Why I'm Hooked

Basketball is the rare sport where one player can change everything and yet nobody wins alone. I grew up playing, I've refereed it, and I'll watch just about any game I can find. Here's what keeps me coming back — and a few of the players who define the game for me.

My favorite sound in sports: the ball going through the net.

There's nothing like the sound of a clean swish or the energy of a buzzer-beater. Below is how I'd rank what makes the sport great, with my reasons nested underneath each one.

What Makes It Great (My Ranking)

  1. The pace
    • Constant back-and-forth action
    • A single fast break can flip the momentum
  2. The skill
    • Shooting, handles, footwork, IQ
    • Seven-footers who can shoot threes
  3. The drama
    • Game-winners and overtime thrillers
    • Playoff series that build for two weeks

Players I'd Build Around

A few of the names I'd take first in a pickup draft of all-time greats:

  • Michael Jordan — the standard everyone is measured against
  • LeBron James — longevity and all-around dominance
  • Stephen Curry — changed how the game is played
  • Kobe Bryant — the blueprint for work ethic

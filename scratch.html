The Beautiful Game of Basketball

Five players, one ball, endless possibility.

Why I'm Hooked

Basketball is the rare sport where one player can change everything and yet nobody wins alone. I grew up playing, I've refereed it, and I'll watch just about any game I can find. Here's what keeps me coming back — and a few of the players who define the game for me.

My favorite sound in sports: the ball going through the net.

There's nothing like the sound of a clean swish or the energy of a buzzer-beater. Below is how I'd rank what makes the sport great, with my reasons nested underneath each one.

What Makes It Great (My Ranking)

  1. The pace
    • Constant back-and-forth action
    • A single fast break can flip the momentum
  2. The skill
    • Shooting, handles, footwork, IQ
    • Seven-footers who can shoot threes
  3. The drama
    • Game-winners and overtime thrillers
    • Playoff series that build for two weeks

My Playing Days

I've always been the smaller guy on the court. In 8th grade I didn't even make the A team — I got beat out by kids I was determined to prove myself against. That stuck with me. I spent the whole offseason working harder than I ever had and walked into freshman year with a chip on my shoulder.

It paid off. I ended up starting for my freshman team — over a bunch of the same guys who'd beaten me out for A team the year before. I didn't stop there. I kept grinding, and by my junior year I'd made Varsity as an undersized guard.

Being smaller forced me to outwork everyone and play smarter — and I wouldn't trade that for anything.

The Players Who Shaped My Game

Favorite of all time

Kobe Bryant

Kobe is the reason "work ethic" is one of my core beliefs. He earned everything he ever got — nothing was handed to him — and he was never satisfied. That killer mentality, the refusal to be broken, the obsession with getting better: that's exactly the mindset I try to model. He outworked everyone in the league for his entire career, and to me that's what made him unbreakable.

Favorite player today

Stephen Curry

Steph is electric — he flat-out changed the game. As an undersized guard myself, I had to get creative, and that's exactly what makes Steph special. He's a huge reason I became a real three-point shooter. My shot was completely self-taught: I'd watch YouTube videos, study his form, then go shoot in my driveway for hours — I even filmed myself to make sure my mechanics looked right. A lot of my shooting form is modeled straight off his.

My GOAT

LeBron James

I have huge respect for Michael Jordan, but for me LeBron is the best to ever do it. Ten Finals appearances — eight in a row — is just absurd. He carried every team he was on, including some that had no business being there, and dragged them to the Finals almost single-handedly. That kind of all-around dominance for that long is what puts him at the top of my list.

The Beautiful Game of Basketball

Five players, one ball, endless possibility.

Why I'm Hooked

Basketball has drawn me in for as long as I can remember — even back when I was no good at it. I grew up grinding away on NBA 2K, and I've always been the kind of fan who watches highlights and NBA news every single day. I follow more basketball and NBA YouTubers than anything else.

What keeps me hooked is the skill the game demands — there are so many different aspects you have to learn. But I also love that you don't have to be the most talented guy to matter: you can be effective and impactful through hustle, defense, and making all the small plays. It's the ultimate team game — you have to learn to work together — but when you're locked in, you can take over and dominate a game all on your own.

My favorite sound in sports: the ball going through the net.

There's nothing like the sound of a clean swish or the energy of a buzzer-beater. Here's what I love most about the game — and why each one matters to me:

My Playing Days

I was never naturally good. I've always been the smaller guy on the court, and in little league I couldn't even make a layup. Everything I can do on a basketball court, I had to work for.

In 8th grade I didn't even make the A team — I got beat out by kids I was determined to prove myself against. That stuck with me. I spent the whole offseason working harder than I ever had and walked into freshman year with a chip on my shoulder. It paid off: I ended up starting for my freshman team over a bunch of the same guys who'd beaten me out. I kept grinding, and by my junior year I'd made Varsity as an undersized guard.

My role has changed at every level. In little league I couldn't do much of anything. By middle school I was fast and athletic, so I made my mark attacking the basket. In high school I became a lockdown defender, a passer, and a three-point shooter. Now in college intramurals — no coach to hold me back, and after years of pickup — I've turned into a go-to scorer. I once dropped 40 in an intramural game with almost 10 threes. Other nights I'm taking over on the offensive glass because I've learned to time my rebounds, then dishing the ball all over the court and racking up assists.

Being smaller forced me to outwork everyone and play smarter — and I wouldn't trade that for anything.

The Players Who Shaped My Game

Favorite of all time

Kobe Bryant

Kobe is the reason "work ethic" is one of my core beliefs. He earned everything he ever got — nothing was handed to him — and he was never satisfied. That killer mentality, the refusal to be broken, the obsession with getting better: that's exactly the mindset I try to model. He outworked everyone in the league for his entire career, and to me that's what made him unbreakable.

Favorite player today

Stephen Curry

Steph is electric — he flat-out changed the game. As an undersized guard myself, I had to get creative, and that's exactly what makes Steph special. He's a huge reason I became a real three-point shooter. My shot was completely self-taught: I'd watch YouTube videos, study his form, then go shoot in my driveway for hours — I even filmed myself to make sure my mechanics looked right. A lot of my shooting form is modeled straight off his.

My GOAT

LeBron James

I have huge respect for Michael Jordan, but for me LeBron is the best to ever do it. Ten Finals appearances — eight in a row — is just absurd. He carried every team he was on, including some that had no business being there, and dragged them to the Finals almost single-handedly. That kind of all-around dominance for that long is what puts him at the top of my list.
